n! means n × (n − 1) × ... × 3 × 2 × 1
For example, 10! = 10 × 9 × ... × 3 × 2 × 1 = 3628800,
and the sum of the digits in the number 10! is 3 + 6 + 2 + 8 + 8 + 0 + 0 = 27.
Find the sum of the digits in the number 100!

I know you are thinking of brute forcing .. haha I knew it. But, how will you handle a long long long integer, too long to be saved in an long long data type. Lets see how you do it.
